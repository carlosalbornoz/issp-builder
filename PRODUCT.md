# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Philippine government agency ICT focal persons and CIOs — often the sole ICT staff member, or one of few, at their agency — who are required by DICT to draft and submit a 3-year Information Systems Strategic Plan (ISSP). They typically do this alongside other duties, under compliance-deadline pressure, and have historically maintained the plan as a dense Word document with a separate budget spreadsheet, manually reconciling the two before every submission.

## Product Purpose

Replace that manual, error-prone Word-and-Excel ISSP drafting workflow with a guided, structured, browser-based editor aligned to the DICT 2026 ISSP template across all four Parts (Agency Profile, Current ICT Assessment, Proposed ICT Strategy, Resource Requirements). Data entered once — budget line items, project details, org information — flows to every place it's needed, so narrative and totals never drift out of sync the way they do in the manual process. Success looks like agencies producing accurate, DICT-aligned ISSPs faster, with less copy-paste reconciliation, and — longer-term — ISSP data becoming structured enough to be publicly comparable in the public interest.

## Positioning

The ISSP tooling DICT hasn't shipped yet: free, local-first, structured, and available now while agencies wait.

## Brand Personality

Sincere, plainspoken, quietly civic. The voice is honest about the bureaucracy the tool exists to ease — it names the problem directly rather than marketing around it — and carries real stakes without being preachy or corporate. Built and offered as a volunteer civic contribution ("para sa bayan"), not a startup pitch.

## Anti-references

Not a sterile government Word-doc clone — it shouldn't feel like the DICT template dressed up as a webpage, all rigid fields and bureaucratic distance. Not a flashy consumer SaaS product either — no gradient-heavy hero metrics, growth-hacked onboarding, or pitch-deck styling. This is serious compliance software that should feel like a real tool, not paperwork and not a product demo.

## Design Principles

Structure prevents drift: data entered once (a budget line, a project name, an org detail) should flow to every place it's needed — narrative sections, computed totals, the exported PDF — so nothing has to be kept in sync by hand.

Show the template, not a form: every field traces back to an actual DICT 2026 requirement. The tool should feel like it's assisting someone through the real document, not abstracting it away into something unrecognizable.

No login, no lock-in: local-first by default, exportable to a portable file, no account wall between an agency and its own planning document.

Compliance software can still be humane: plainspoken labels, visible consequences for every action, and honest affordances — no decorative control that doesn't do what it looks like it does.

Built by someone who's filled this form: defaults, validation, and guided structure come from having actually drafted ISSPs under deadline pressure, not designed from the outside looking in.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline. No additional formal requirements beyond that at this time; contrast, keyboard operability, and labeling should meet AA as a matter of course.
