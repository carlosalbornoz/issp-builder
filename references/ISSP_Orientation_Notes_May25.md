## 1. What Is an ISSP?

The **Information Systems Strategic Plan (ISSP)** is a **3-year ICT framework** for a government agency that serves as its blueprint and roadmap for ICT plans, programs, and digitalization efforts. It is the basis for ICT budget recommendations submitted to DBM through MITHI. [~35:12, Sir Mark Evans]

### Objectives [~39:00–41:00, Sir Mark Evans]
1. Rationalize the use of ICT to improve and accelerate delivery of government services to stakeholders.
2. Rationalize the allocation of government funds — ensure every peso of taxpayer money has maximum impact, with no duplication.

### Who Must Submit [~41:00–43:00, Sir Mark Evans]
- Mandatory for all **National Government Agencies (NGAs)**, **GOCCs**, and **SUCs** per GAA (Section 27, FY 2026 GAA), Department Circular HRA-001, and MITHI resolutions.
- **LGUs** are covered by RA 12254 (E-Governance Act) and highly encouraged to comply; ISSP submission is not yet explicitly mandatory for LGUs under the IRR. [~42:00, Sir Mark Evans: "For LGUs naman po, although wala po LGUs dito, tinihimok din silang gumawa"]
- **GOCCs under GCG** that do not receive DBM subsidies are still required to submit per DBM Corporate Budget Circular, DICT DC 001 (2024), MITHI Advisory 2025-02, MITHI Resolution 2026-02, and GCG MC 2024-01. [Handout FAQ #5]
- Agencies with **fiscal autonomy** — submission is highly encouraged but not explicitly mandated. [Handout FAQ #6]
- **Local Water Districts** — required per DBM Corporate Budget Circular No. 25 (24 Oct 2023). [Handout FAQ #7]
- Regional offices must NOT submit directly to DICT; they submit through their **Central Office**, which includes them in the TWG. [Handout FAQ #9, #10]

## 3. Key Changes in the New Template (2026 Version)

> All items in this section from [~55:57–01:01:34, Sir Mark Evans] unless noted otherwise.

| What Was Removed/Simplified | What Was Added |
|---|---|

## 4. Pre-Planning Requirements

> All items in this section from [~55:57–01:01:34, Sir Mark Evans] unless noted otherwise.

Before drafting the ISSP:

1. **Designate a CIO and ISSP Focal Person** [~56:00] — CIO leads ICT governance and digital transformation; focal person is the day-to-day ISSP coordinator who DICT contacts. [~56:15: "Si ICSP focal person naman, siya po yung kukuliti namin in relation po to your ICSP submission"]
2. **Form a Technical Working Group (TWG)** [~57:00] — must include unit/division heads (at least senior level) so ICT requirements from all units are captured. [~57:30: "we recommend to include, if possible, yung mga unit fields para po ma-insure na yung mga ISP requirements for all units are taken into account"] Should include the CIO/focal, planning officer, ICT officer, budget officer, and unit representatives.
3. **Designate a Secretariat** [~58:00] — handles logistics and documentation.
4. **Conduct pre-planning workshops** [~58:30] — gather data via surveys, interviews, group discussions, observation.
5. **Prepare a work plan** [~59:00] — set deadlines, assign tasks.

**TWG Composition (Q&A clarification)** [~02:50:00, Sir Mark Evans]: "Should be led by the CIO or the IS planner... members should be composed by the division heads or section heads. At least senior level para lang ma-provide nila yung ICT requirements ng kanilang mga units."
